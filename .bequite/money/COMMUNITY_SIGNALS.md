## Methodology

Claude captures signals using available research tools (WebFetch / WebSearch / Chrome MCP / Computer Use MCP). **Public content only** — no auth-walled or private content; no DM scraping.

Per BeQuite safety rules:
- ❌ Don't capture scammy claims (mark + skip + exclude from OPPORTUNITIES.md)
- ❌ Don't capture VPN-misrepresentation tutorials
- ❌ Don't capture CAPTCHA-bypass / fake-account / account-renting discussions
- ❌ Don't capture upfront-fee schemes
- ✅ Capture legitimate payout proof + community-verified opportunities
- ✅ Capture "this paid out for me last week" testimony (verify with multiple sources)
- ✅ Mark uncertainty clearly
- ✅ Cite source URL + date so user can verify

---

## Why community signals matter

Make-money platforms often have **lag** between launch and search-engine indexing. Communities surface them first.

Examples:
- A new AI training task platform pays $20-30/hour but only 200 people know about it because it just launched
- A regional research panel pays well in Eastern Europe but the website is in Polish and isn't ranked in Google English
- A creator-tool startup pays testers $50 per session but only announced it in a Twitter thread

Without community-signal scanning, these don't show up. With it, they do.
