# Offer Engine — `/bq-offer` (C11) — alpha.23

Turn a skill, service, automation, product idea, course idea, niche, or pain point into a **sellable productized offer** — the standing package a buyer can say yes to, distinct from C8 `/bq-proposal` (the per-client pitch of that package).

## Position in the monetization chain

```
C6 pain-radar (find verified pains) → C10 make-money (match to earning tracks)
→ C11 /bq-offer (package the standing product)
→ C8 proposal (pitch it per-client) → W4.2 release proof (turn delivery into evidence)
```

The offer answers, in artifacts: who is this for · what painful problem · what promised outcome · what's included · **what's excluded** · what proof is needed · what pricing tiers · what guarantee is *safe* · what to ask at onboarding · what to demo · what proposal angle · what command runs next.

## Outputs — `.bequite/offers/` (12 files per offer)

OFFER (assembled 5-element message: promise + price + bonus + guarantee + differentiator) · TARGET_CLIENT (specific segment with buying power, never "everyone") · PAIN_AND_OUTCOME (painful specific problem + measurable outcome + cost of inaction) · DELIVERABLES (included + explicit exclusions) · PRICING_TIERS (2–3 tiers, anchoring, fixed-vs-retainer, MENA purchasing-power awareness) · GUARANTEE_AND_RISK_REVERSAL (seller-honorable terms, separated from the promise) · ONBOARDING_QUESTIONS (feeds `/bq-scope intake`) · OUTREACH_MESSAGE (hook→pain→offer→CTA in the user's voice) · DEMO_IDEA (5-minute capability proof) · PROOF_CHECKLIST (evidence have-vs-need; feeds `/bq-release proof`) · PROPOSAL_ANGLE (feeds C8) · NEXT_STEPS.

## Hard rules (refusal-grade)

No overpromising · no invented market demand (claims trace to pain-radar/user facts/live research or are marked `UNVERIFIED ASSUMPTION`) · no fake income claims · **promise ≠ guarantee** (guarantee covers only what the seller controls; results-guarantees refused) · no legal advice (contract/guarantee wording flagged for professional review) · no claimed experience absent from memory/user input · confidence per section · proof gaps listed. Vague input ⇒ 3–5 high-value questions OR clearly-assumption-marked draft.

## Reference grounding

Inherits the Course Engine's verified Reference A discipline where it overlaps (one painful specific problem · 5-element offer message tested on real people · bonuses solving side-problems · risk-reversal psychology · MVP-first: sell the first version small before overbuilding) plus productized-service practice: fixed scope beats hourly ambiguity · exclusions prevent scope creep · tiered pricing anchors decisions · demos beat descriptions · proof compounds (every delivery feeds the next offer's PROOF_CHECKLIST).

## Language rule

Output language = the user's requested language (Arabic ⇒ Arabic pack with localization-rtl + MENA pricing context; English ⇒ English). Source-material language never forces output language; concepts translate, not literal phrases.
